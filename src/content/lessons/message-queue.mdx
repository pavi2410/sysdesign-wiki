## What it is

A message queue stores units of work until consumers are ready to process them. Producers enqueue messages, consumers dequeue and handle them, and the queue provides buffering between the two.

## Why it matters

Queues decouple slow or unreliable work from user-facing requests. They smooth traffic spikes, enable retries, and let workers scale independently from the API.

## How it works

A producer publishes a message with a payload and metadata. A worker claims it, processes it, and acknowledges completion. If the worker fails, the message can become visible again or move to a dead-letter queue.

## When to use it

Use queues for emails, webhooks, media processing, fan-out, notifications, imports, and anything too slow for the request path.

## Tradeoffs / failure modes

Queues introduce eventual consistency, duplicate delivery, ordering questions, and backlogs. Consumers should be idempotent.

## Tiny example

After a video upload, the API enqueues a transcode job and returns immediately while workers create HLS renditions.

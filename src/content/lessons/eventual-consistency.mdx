## What it is

Eventual consistency means writes may not be visible everywhere immediately, but replicas should converge to the same value if no new writes occur.

## Why it matters

Distributed systems often choose availability and low latency over immediate global agreement. Feeds, counters, search indexes, analytics, and replicated stores commonly accept temporary staleness.

## How it works

Writes propagate asynchronously through logs, events, replication streams, or background jobs. Reads from different replicas can return different values until propagation catches up.

## When to use it

Use it when the product can tolerate stale reads or delayed side effects, especially across regions or high-volume derived views.

## Tradeoffs / failure modes

Users can see missing updates, out-of-order events, or stale permissions. The product needs reconciliation, conflict handling, and clear boundaries for what must be strongly consistent.

## Tiny example

A tweet appears on the author's profile instantly but reaches every follower feed over the next few seconds.

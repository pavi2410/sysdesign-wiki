## What it is

Distributed tracing records the path of a request through multiple services as a tree of spans.

## Why it matters

Modern requests often cross gateways, services, caches, queues, and databases. Tracing shows where time is spent and where failures start.

## How it works

A trace ID is created at the edge and propagated through headers or message metadata. Each service creates spans for its work and exports timing, attributes, and errors.

## When to use it

Use tracing for microservices, APIs with latency SLOs, complex fan-out, background workflows, and third-party integrations.

## Tradeoffs / failure modes

Sampling can hide rare issues, missing propagation breaks traces, and high-cardinality attributes can become expensive.

## Tiny example

A slow checkout trace shows 30ms in the API, 80ms in inventory, and 1.8s waiting on a payment provider.

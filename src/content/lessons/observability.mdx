## What it is

Observability is the ability to understand system behavior using signals such as metrics, logs, traces, events, profiles, and alerts.

## Why it matters

You cannot operate what you cannot see. Observability helps engineers detect incidents, debug regressions, understand performance, and validate product behavior.

## How it works

Services emit structured events and measurements with consistent identifiers. Dashboards, alerts, and tracing tools connect those signals across requests and services.

## When to use it

Use observability from the first production release. Add deeper instrumentation around critical user paths, dependencies, queues, background jobs, and SLOs.

## Tradeoffs / failure modes

Too much noisy telemetry costs money and hides real problems. Too little leaves teams guessing during incidents.

## Tiny example

An API request includes a trace ID that appears in gateway logs, service spans, database timing, and error reports.

## What it is

Transport Layer Security encrypts data in transit and lets clients verify they are talking to the intended server.

## Why it matters

Without TLS, attackers on the network can read or modify traffic. TLS protects logins, APIs, uploads, cookies, tokens, and user data as it crosses the internet.

## How it works

During the handshake, the server presents a certificate, both sides agree on cryptographic parameters, and they derive session keys for encrypted communication.

## When to use it

Use TLS for all public HTTP traffic, internal service traffic when networks are untrusted, webhooks, OAuth redirects, and custom domains.

## Tradeoffs / failure modes

Certificate renewal, hostname validation, mixed content, old protocol versions, and private key handling are common operational issues.

## Tiny example

A custom domain points to your SaaS, which automatically provisions a certificate and serves the tenant's site over HTTPS.
